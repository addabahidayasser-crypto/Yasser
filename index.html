Explore drills, common mistakes, and training tips for all swimming strokes.

Freestyle (Crawl)

Focus on head position, arm movement, and body alignment.

  • Swim with a frontal snorkel – focus on alignment and propulsion
  • Opposition arm kicks – develop roll and breathing placement
  • Crawl rattrapé – practice arm positioning and acceleration
  • Pull buoy between ankles – improves body alignment
  • Kicking drills with board – strengthens legs
  • Single-arm crawl – refine technique on each side
  • Pause drills – improve balance and acceleration
  • Crawl with paddles (light) – refine hand placement

Backstroke

Maintain stable body alignment, arm rotation, and kick rhythm.

  • Kick with fins – increase propulsion and ankle flexibility
  • Rolled kick drills – improve shoulder roll coordination
  • Opposition drills – body alignment and roll timing
  • Backstroke “catch and pull” – refine arm catch

Breaststroke

Coordinate arm and leg movements, focus on glide and timing.

  • Leg movement against the wall – perfect foot and knee orientation
  • Back-on-back legs – strengthen leg control
  • Leg-only with board – combine kick with breathing
  • Exaggerated glide after kick – emphasize efficient glide
  • Pause drills – improve arm and breathing coordination
  • Brasse with tennis ball – enhance head and body alignment awareness

Butterfly

Focus on powerful undulating motion, synchronized breathing, and strong kick.

  • Underwater body undulations – develop leg power and rhythm
  • Head-lead undulations – proper body motion as arms enter
  • Duck dive – correct body stabilization
  • Single-arm butterfly – focus on pulse timing and coordination
  • Alternate drills with/without board – integrate motion with kick propulsion
  • Vertical undulations – improve leg thrust and timing

General Tips

  • Keep your body horizontal in water for better efficiency
  • Control your breathing, especially in butterfly and freestyle
  • Stay relaxed to reduce fatigue and improve glide
  • Practice drills progressively to isolate technique before full strokes
  • Count your arm cycles per length to measure rhythm stability
  • Use boards, paddles, or fins as recommended for targeted drills
  • Review and correct common errors (NatationPourTous.com advice)
